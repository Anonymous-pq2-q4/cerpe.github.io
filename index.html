CERPE enables communication-efficient relative pose estimation for connected autonomous vehicles encountering each other at urban intersections, handling intermittent visual overlap and limited bandwidth.

Abstract

Relative pose estimation is a fundamental capability for collaborative perception and coordination in multi-robot systems. However, robots encountering each other in real-world environments often operate in short interaction windows and must operate under limited communication bandwidth with intermittent or missing visual overlap caused by occlusions or limited fields of view. Existing approaches typically rely on global reference frames, assume sustained view overlap, or incur prohibitive communication costs, thereby limiting their applicability to ephemeral collaborative perception.

To address these challenges, we introduce communication-efficient relative pose estimation (CERPE) that integrates vision foundation models to jointly estimate ego-motion and inter-robot relative pose. Communication efficiency is achieved by decoupling overlap detection from pose estimation, using compact descriptors for continuous inter-robot communication and selectively exchanging key raw observations only during overlapping encounters. Non-overlapping encounters are handled by propagating inter-robot relative poses through metrically scaled ego-motion, thus maintaining relative pose estimates even in the absence of visual overlap.

Experiments in simulation and real-world robots demonstrate that CERPE achieves robust 6-DoF pose estimation in ephemeral collaborative perception.

Method Overview

CERPE restructures vision foundation models to jointly estimate metrically scaled ego-motion and inter-robot relative poses under communication constraints. Each robot encodes its observations into compressed visual descriptors using SALAD and continuously shares them to detect visual overlap. When overlap is detected, raw observations are selectively exchanged, and VGGT estimates the inter-robot relative pose. To resolve monocular scale ambiguity, we align predicted depth with metric depth from Metric3Dv2. During non-overlapping periods, relative poses are propagated using metrically scaled ego-motion.

Experimental Results

We evaluate CERPE across three settings: (1) a high-fidelity CARLA simulation with 71 encounter scenarios, (2) real-world connected autonomous driving trials from OpenMars, and (3) physical robot teams with 3 Agilex Limo Pro robots.

Physical Robot Teams

We validate CERPE on physical robot teams consisting of 3 Agilex Limo Pro robots across 13 indoor scenarios with 382 pairwise image pairs. CERPE runs in 438ms for 2 robots and 780ms for 3 robots, demonstrating real-time capability.
